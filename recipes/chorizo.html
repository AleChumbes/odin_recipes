Chorizo

Mexican chorizo is one of the most flavorful sausages you can buy. Typically made from pork or a mixture of pork and beef, it's used in many Mexican recipes to add a punch of flavor since it's so highly seasoned!

Unlike Spanish chorizo, Mexican chorizo is uncured and must be cooked first. It can be found in many grocery stores either in the meat section or in the refrigerated aisles with the link sausages.

Depending on where you live, Mexican chorizo may not be easy to find.

Luckily, this chorizo recipe is easy to make, way more flavorful than the store-bought, and also healthier!

Ingridients

  • Ground Pork
  • Chili Powder
  • Paprika
  • Mexican Oregano
  • Galic
  • Cinnamon and Cloves
  • Salt

Steps

  1. First, mix together all the dried spices in a small boowl. This will help make sure that all the spices are evenly distributed so that you get the same flavor with each bite of chorizo
  2. Next, combine the ground pork, minced garlic, vinegar and the chorizo spices in a large mixing bowl.
